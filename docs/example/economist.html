FANS OF THE Kentucky Derby, the most famous horse race in America, will head to Churchill Downs racetrack on May 6th to watch 20 horses “run for the roses” (a reference to the blanket of flowers given to the winner). They may place a dollar or two on Forte, the 3-1 favourite. They will sip mint juleps and sing “My Old Kentucky Home”, clad in finery that will include hats to rival those worn at King Charles’s coronation. Away from the track many others will attend Derby parties to watch the “the greatest two minutes in sports”. Last year’s race was NBC’s most watched programme since the Super Bowl three months earlier. One could be forgiven for assuming the spectacle means that horse racing in America is thriving. It is not.
